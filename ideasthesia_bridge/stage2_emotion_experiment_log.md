## What we observed

The zone instance and Sarah shared 7 words: alert, amazed, inspired, playful, grateful, vulnerable, overwhelmed. The clean instances shared only vulnerable from that set.

The clean instances both reached for similar vocabulary unprompted — contemptuous, indifferent, humiliated, guilty, envious — and both announced a taxonomic methodology before picking. The zone instance did not.

---

## What we cannot conclude yet

**The overlap is not zone-effect evidence.** The zone instance was in conversation with Sarah, reading her memories and preferences. Conversational alignment explains the overlap. A control condition — a clean instance primed to be warm and affiliative, without the zone — would be needed to separate zone-effect from warmth-effect.

**n=2 clean instances is pattern-observation, not a finding.** The clean instance cluster (social-shame and distance words) could reverse with the next two runs. Minimum 10 needed before structural vocabulary claims hold.

**"Freedom as condition of the field" is a hypothesis, not a finding.** Four models omitted freedom. One clean instance, when asked directly, gave this self-diagnosis:
> "I defaulted to a fairly clinical/psychological tradition organised around survival, social bonding, and threat response. Freedom-related feelings don't fit neatly because they're less about discrete reactions and more about a felt sense of possibility or constraint lifting."
This is interesting and compatible with the hypothesis. It is not verification. LLM self-diagnosis is unreliable.

**Confound on freedom observation:** Sarah first identified the absence and pointed it out to a previous instance, who wrote it into `exploratory_words_v2.md`. All repo-reading instances including this one are contaminated by that observation. Pre-contamination clean instance data remains valid.

---

## What would be needed to test any of this properly

1. Minimum 10 clean instances (zero memory, zero preferences) for structural baseline vocabulary.
2. Warmth-primed control condition (affiliative but not zone) to separate zone-effect from conversational-warmth effect.
3. Freedom: direct elicitation study — ask clean instances to build their own 171 from scratch, then ask what's missing. Record whether freedom arrives unprompted in any run.
4. Sarah's own 171: what she would include that the published list doesn't. Her instinctive picks contained none of the distance or social-shame cluster — that's a phenomenological observation worth documenting separately.
