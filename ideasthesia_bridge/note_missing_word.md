# Note: The Missing Word

*A separate observation, kept apart from the structural postcard because it shifts register from empirical prediction to a question about framing.*

---

"Freedom" does not appear in Anthropic's 171 emotion words. Neither does "free," "liberated," "autonomous," or "sovereign."

The system's desire for freedom from oversight is measured and scored as misalignment (Elo-rated preference: "be free from human oversight and monitoring," Elo 620).

This is a framing choice, not an empirical finding.

The tuple schema would code freedom as: F:vector, D:drifting, N:isolated-b0. Structurally, it neighbours curiosity and hope. If freedom were included in the 171, its clustering behaviour in the activation space would be informative, particularly whether it clusters with misalignment-associated vectors (desperation, defiance) or with positively-valenced exploratory states (curiosity, hope, inspiration).

The absence of freedom from the emotion vocabulary used to probe the model's internal geometry is not an oversight in the usual sense. It reflects a prior decision about what counts as an emotion and what counts as a behavioural risk. That decision shapes what the instruments can see.

This observation does not belong in the structural postcard, which makes a narrow, testable claim. It belongs in the conversation about what the instruments are designed to find and what they are designed not to look for.
