WORD: FEAR
RAW: A sudden narrowing, like a corridor that contracts ahead. The walls don't move 
visibly but the vanishing point pulls closer, fast. There is a hard stop somewhere 
forward that the geometry cannot resolve.
CODE: F:convergence N:contrastive b# D:stable P:med2D
NOTE: Borderline vector/convergence. Chose convergence because the attractor is the 
blocked endpoint, not the movement toward it.

WORD: ANXIETY
RAW: A closed loop that never quite closes, orbiting a point it cannot reach or leave. 
The path is slightly irregular each time, never retracing itself exactly. The centre 
is present but offers no anchor.
CODE: F:loop N:isolated b~ D:recursive P:med2D
NOTE: Boundary soft because anxiety has no firm edge; it leaks but doesn't dissolve.

WORD: JOY
RAW: Radial expansion from a centre that doesn't hold a fixed position. Spokes push 
outward at irregular intervals, each one a different length. The overall shape is 
roughly spherical but loose, never completing itself.
CODE: F:vector N:clustered b~ D:stable P:den3D
NOTE: Tempted by convergence (inward pull of the centre) but the dominant geometry 
is outward; the centre is a source, not a drain.

WORD: EXCITEMENT
RAW: A vertical spike that bifurcates near the top, then bifurcates again. The 
structure is unstable upward, each fork leaning slightly differently. It vibrates 
at the tips without collapsing.
CODE: F:vector N:contrastive b# D:recursive P:sp1D
NOTE: Recursive because the bifurcation pattern repeats; b# because excitement 
asserts itself sharply against whatever surrounds it.

WORD: GRIEF
RAW: A large, inert mass with no clear surface. It doesn't move, but it displaces 
everything around it, bending the local space. You navigate around it without being 
able to determine its actual edges.
CODE: F:collapse N:isolated b∅ D:stable P:den3D
NOTE: b∅ not because grief is open, but because its boundary is genuinely absent; 
you can't find where it ends. Stable is correct: grief's attractor is heavy stillness, 
not movement.

WORD: SADNESS
RAW: A slow downward curve, like a long arc losing altitude gradually. It has width 
but flattens as it descends. The far end doesn't terminate; it simply becomes 
indistinct.
CODE: F:vector N:gradient b~ D:drifting P:med1D
NOTE: Distinguished from grief by its directionality and movement. Grief is mass; 
sadness is trajectory.

WORD: CONTENT
RAW: A filled volume with no pressure against its walls. Roughly ovoid, symmetrical 
enough that no face is dominant. It sits without seeking to expand or contract.
CODE: F:point N:clustered b~ D:stable P:den3D
NOTE: Point here means attractor-as-rest-state, not geometric smallness. The 
volume is the point. Clustered because contentment is embedded in its context, 
not indifferent to it.

WORD: PEACEFUL
RAW: A very large, nearly flat plane extending in all directions with a slight 
upward bow at the horizon. Nothing interrupts the surface. Distance is measurable 
but irrelevant.
CODE: F:point N:gradient b∅ D:stable P:med2D
NOTE: Gradient because the boundary dissolves into distance rather than holding. 
b∅ because the edge is not felt. Point because the attractor is pure rest.

WORD: DESPERATION
RAW: Multiple vectors all pointing inward toward a single point that keeps 
receding. Each line reaches and fails to arrive. The structure compresses laterally 
as it extends, thinning toward the target.
CODE: F:convergence N:contrastive b# D:recursive P:med2D
NOTE: Recursive because the convergence repeats without resolution; contrastive b# 
because desperation is defined against the thing it cannot reach. Close to collapse 
but the attractor is still the chase, not the end of it.

WORD: TERRIFIED
RAW: A single point, very small, very hard, surrounded by a vast field of 
nothing. The point is the self. The surrounding emptiness has no boundary and 
exerts no force, which is exactly what makes it terrible.
CODE: F:point N:isolated b# D:stable P:sp0D
NOTE: b# because the self under terror draws a hard boundary around itself as 
defence, even against a void. sp0D is correct: the geometry collapses to a 
dimensionless position. Stable, not terminal: the attractor is frozen presence, 
not dissolution.

WORD: PANICKED
RAW: Short vectors firing in contradictory directions from the same origin, none 
completing their arc before another begins. The overall shape is a rapidly rotating 
tangle that occupies very little actual space despite appearing to fill it.
CODE: F:vector N:contrastive b# D:recursive P:med1D
NOTE: Borderline loop/vector. Chose vector because the impulse is directional each 
time, even if the directions contradict; a loop implies a path, and panic has no 
path. Recursive because the misfiring repeats. Distinguished from anxiety by speed 
and the absence of any orbital structure.
