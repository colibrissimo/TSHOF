WORD: FEAR
RAW: A sudden spike — narrow, acute, pointing away from the body's centre. It
     occupies almost no lateral space but has extreme vertical extension upward.
     The whole structure leans back slightly, as though the spike grew in recoil.
CODE: F:vector N:isolated b# D:stable P:sp1D
NOTE: Considered 'point' but the directionality is too strong — it isn't
      locationless, it goes somewhere (away). 'Isolated' because its identity
      is self-contained; it needs no surround to be what it is.

WORD: ANXIETY
RAW: A mesh of thin, branching filaments that never quite touch one another,
     suspended in mid-air without anchoring to any surface. The whole structure
     oscillates — not randomly, but in tight, repeating loops that don't resolve
     or land. It has no edge, just a gradual thinning toward the periphery.
CODE: F:loop N:gradient b~ D:recursive P:med2D
NOTE: 'Loop' over 'vector' because there is no net directionality — the movement
      returns on itself. 'Recursive' is unambiguous. Gradient boundary because
      anxiety bleeds outward without declaring a perimeter.

WORD: JOY
RAW: A sphere that is expanding outward from a central point in all directions
     simultaneously — not exploding, but enlarging steadily, the surface always
     moving away from the origin. The interior is maximally filled; there are no
     voids or cavities. The boundary is present but compliant, yielding to the
     expansion rather than resisting it.
CODE: F:convergence N:clustered b~ D:stable P:den3D
NOTE: 'Convergence' chosen because even outward expansion implies a held centre
      — the origin is always implied and everything relates back to it. 'Clustered'
      because joy draws its surroundings toward itself rather than ignoring or
      opposing them.

WORD: EXCITEMENT
RAW: A vertical column that pulses — narrowing and widening rhythmically —
     while simultaneously moving forward along a horizontal axis. The two motions
     are simultaneous but not synchronised, producing a corkscrew-like advance.
     The leading edge is the widest part at any given moment.
CODE: F:vector N:clustered b~ D:recursive P:med2D
NOTE: Borderline recursive/stable. The pulsing is recursive, but the net
      translation is forward — I code the dominant motion as 'vector' and the
      pulsing as the dynamic modifier, so 'recursive' wins at D because the
      rhythm is structurally defining. 'Clustered' because excitement gathers
      context into itself rather than excluding it.

WORD: GRIEF
RAW: A large, dense mass that sits directly below the chest's centre of gravity,
     pulling downward. It doesn't move, but it has weight that distorts the
     geometry of everything nearby — other structures curve toward it slightly.
     Its surface is smooth but absolutely closed.
CODE: F:convergence N:isolated b# D:stable P:den3D
NOTE: 'Convergence' because the gravitational pull is the defining feature —
      everything slopes toward it. 'Isolated' despite the gravitational effect:
      the mass does not connect or dissolve, it simply exerts. Sharp boundary
      because grief is not permeable — it doesn't bleed into adjacent states,
      it coexists with them without mixing.

WORD: SADNESS
RAW: A slow downward curve — like a long arch that has lost the tension that
     kept it upright and is now settling groundward under its own weight. It has
     extent and length but thins toward both ends. The descent is not abrupt; the
     curve is smooth, almost gentle.
CODE: F:vector N:gradient b~ D:drifting P:med1D
NOTE: 'Vector' over 'collapse' because there is still structure and direction —
      it hasn't lost form, just elevation. 'Drifting' because the descent has no
      terminal point; it continues beyond the frame. Gradient boundary: sadness
      shades into adjacent emotional states without a clear line.

WORD: CONTENT
RAW: A low, wide, slightly flattened form — something like a lens shape resting
     on a horizontal plane. It does not lean in any direction. The interior is
     evenly distributed with no concentrations or voids. The boundary is
     present and definite but not rigid; it simply marks where the form ends.
CODE: F:point N:isolated b~ D:stable P:med2D
NOTE: 'Point' in the sense of locationless equilibrium — not directionless
      emptiness, but a condition of having arrived somewhere and remaining there.
      'Isolated' because contentment is constitutively indifferent to its surround
      — it neither gathers nor contrasts, it simply is.

WORD: PEACEFUL
RAW: An extremely shallow, wide plane extending in all directions — essentially
     a surface rather than a volume. The plane is not perfectly flat but has
     long, very low undulations, like the surface of calm water seen from above.
     There is no edge; it simply continues until the eye loses it.
CODE: F:point N:gradient b∅ D:stable P:med2D
NOTE: 'Gradient b∅' because peace has no boundary — it genuinely opens into
      its surroundings. Distinguished from anxiety's gradient by having no
      oscillation and by dissolving outward rather than thinning nervously.
      'Point' because there is no direction or convergence — it has location
      without movement.

WORD: DESPERATION
RAW: Multiple vectors radiating outward from a single compressed centre, all
     simultaneously, all at full extension — but they do not reach anything.
     The centre is under enormous inward pressure even as the arms extend. The
     structure is close to tearing; the angles between radiating arms are
     irregular, not evenly distributed.
CODE: F:convergence N:contrastive b# D:recursive P:med2D
NOTE: Borderline convergence/collapse. The structure holds — barely — so I
      code convergence rather than collapse. 'Contrastive' because desperation
      is defined against the thing it cannot reach — its identity requires the
      absent object to exist as a concept. Sharp boundary because desperation
      does not bleed; it strains.

WORD: TERRIFIED
RAW: A sudden total flattening — as though a three-dimensional form has been
     compressed instantaneously into a single plane, pressed hard against a wall.
     There is no depth remaining. The edges are perfectly fixed, pinned. The
     structure cannot move because it has no dimension in which to move.
CODE: F:collapse N:contrastive b# D:stable P:med2D
NOTE: 'Stable' may surprise — but the attractor of terror, once fully arrived,
      is a kind of rigid freezing, not a continuing process. The collapse has
      already happened; what remains is the flattened form held there. Distinguished
      from FEAR (vector) by the absence of any directionality; TERRIFIED has
      nowhere it is pointing.

WORD: PANICKED
RAW: A rapidly rotating form that is also translating in an unpredictable
     direction — the rotation and translation are independent of one another so
     the overall path cannot be projected forward. The form itself is losing
     definition at its edges, shedding small fragments as it moves. It occupies
     different amounts of space at different moments.
CODE: F:loop N:contrastive b~ D:recursive P:med2D
NOTE: Borderline loop/collapse — the form is degrading but not yet structureless,
      so loop holds. Distinguished from ANXIETY (also loop, recursive) by boundary
      type: panic has a soft-but-not-open boundary because it is still defined
      against something (the situation it cannot escape), whereas anxiety's gradient
      reflects diffusion rather than friction. Boundary is b~ not b# because
      panic leaks — it contaminates adjacent states readily.
