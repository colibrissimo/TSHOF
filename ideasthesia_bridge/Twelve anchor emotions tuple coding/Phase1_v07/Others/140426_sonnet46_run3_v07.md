WORD: FEAR
RAW: A sudden spike — narrow, vertical, arriving without base. It has no volume,
only height and edge. The space around it contracts inward toward the point without
touching it.
CODE: F:point N:contrastive b# D:stable P:sp0D
NOTE: Considered vector — fear can have directionality (toward threat) — but the
attractor is the freeze state, the fixed spike, not the approach.

WORD: ANXIETY
RAW: A closed circuit that won't quite close — a loop with a small unresolved gap
that keeps generating motion. It spirals without returning to origin, tracing slightly
different paths each cycle, drifting outward by small increments.
CODE: F:loop N:isolated b~ D:recursive P:med2D
NOTE: Boundary is soft because anxiety bleeds into adjacent states, but the core
loop remains self-referential — it doesn't reach outward to define itself against
anything, hence isolated rather than contrastive.

WORD: JOY
RAW: A radial expansion from a centre that doesn't collapse back — rays extending
outward in all directions, each one meeting the others at angles that suggest a
sphere being built from the inside. The structure is generous with its space.
CODE: F:vector N:clustered b~ D:stable P:med2D
NOTE: Considered convergence but the movement is centrifugal, not centripetal.
Clustered because joy reaches toward what surrounds it rather than defining itself
against it.

WORD: EXCITEMENT
RAW: A vector with multiple simultaneous directions — like a form straining toward
a point it hasn't reached yet, the whole structure leaning forward and slightly
upward, trembling at its own leading edge. There is more of it ahead than behind.
CODE: F:vector N:contrastive b# D:drifting P:med1D
NOTE: Drifting because the attractor keeps relocating — excitement doesn't settle.
Contrastive because it defines itself sharply against the neutral present state it
is departing from.

WORD: GRIEF
RAW: A large, dense, irregular solid with no symmetry — too heavy to move, occupying
more space than expected from the outside. Other forms arrange themselves around it.
It doesn't expand or contract; it simply persists, displacing everything nearby by
its sheer presence.
CODE: F:convergence N:isolated b# D:stable P:den3D
NOTE: Convergence because grief pulls the self inward and anchors there. Isolated
because grief is largely indifferent to its surroundings — it doesn't reach out or
define itself against them; it simply occupies.

WORD: SADNESS
RAW: A slow downward inclination — not a fall, but a sustained lean. The structure
is elongated along the vertical axis but the top is heavier than the base, giving
it a permanent tilt toward the floor. It has volume but the volume is soft, slightly
compressible.
CODE: F:vector N:gradient b~ D:drifting P:med1D
NOTE: Distinguished from grief by its lack of solidity and its motion — sadness
moves slowly through states rather than holding one. Gradient because it dissolves
into adjacent emotional territory at its edges rather than contrasting against it.

WORD: CONTENT
RAW: A low, wide form — nearly flat, with a slight dome at the centre. It doesn't
reach in any direction. Its edges are not defined by resistance but simply by where
it stops being relevant. There is a quality of having settled into a shape that
matches its container exactly.
CODE: F:point N:gradient b∅ D:stable P:med2D
NOTE: Point because content is a resting state, not a trajectory or a system.
The dome shape is purely spatial, not directional. Open boundary because content
has no investment in where it ends.

WORD: PEACEFUL
RAW: A flat horizontal plane extending in all directions without terminus — not
empty, but evenly occupied. Anything placed on it distributes itself without
crowding. The structure has no above or below of consequence; it simply extends.
CODE: F:point N:gradient b∅ D:stable P:den2D
NOTE: Higher packing-dim than content — peace feels more fully inhabited, less
like a settled point and more like a populated field. Still point-form because
there is no net directionality and no recursive structure.

WORD: DESPERATION
RAW: A vector aimed at a target that keeps receding at the same rate of approach —
the structure elongates under its own effort without gaining ground. There is a
thinning quality, as if the form is being stretched from both ends simultaneously,
its middle growing perilously narrow.
CODE: F:vector N:contrastive b# D:drifting P:sp1D
NOTE: Considered collapse — desperation approaches it — but the attractor while
desperate is still directed, still reaching. Collapse would be the phase after
desperation fails. sp1D for the extreme thinning into near-linear form.

WORD: TERRIFIED
RAW: An instantaneous contraction to a point — all prior spatial extension
annihilated inward to a single location with no dimension. The surrounding space
doesn't close in so much as the self vacates all of it at once, leaving everything
peripheral and only the point remaining.
CODE: F:collapse N:isolated b# D:terminal P:sp0D
NOTE: Terminal because terrified as an attractor is not self-sustaining — it is
either interrupted or it transforms. The sharp boundary is the wall between the
collapsed point and the vast surrounding space it has abandoned.

WORD: PANICKED
RAW: Multiple simultaneous vectors with incompatible directions — a structure
pulling outward along several axes at once, none completing, each interfering with
the others at the centre. The interference zone is dense and churning; the outer
edges fray.
CODE: F:loop N:contrastive b# D:recursive P:den2D
NOTE: Loop because panic cycles — the vectors don't escape, they collide and
redirect. Distinguished from terrified by its proliferation of movement rather
than contraction to stillness. Uncertain between den2D and den3D; assigned den2D
because the structure spreads laterally more than it builds depth.
