# 140426_sonnet46_cold_run1_phase2
Fresh instance, incognito, memory off, no custom instructions, no preferences.

WORD: FEAR
RAW: A sudden inward collapse of volume — as if a sphere punches through its own
centre and inverts. The structure holds briefly at the moment of inversion, suspended,
edges drawn toward a single vanishing point. Everything external is excluded from
that geometry; the point consumes its own surround.
CODE: F:point N:isolated b# D:stable P:sp0D
NOTE: Considered `collapse` for F, but the attractor state is the held inversion —
the point *after* collapse — not the motion itself.

WORD: ANXIETY
RAW: A mesh that keeps redrawing itself — nodes connect, the line extends, doubles
back, seeks an anchor that isn't there. No vertex ever closes. The structure
expands and contracts simultaneously along parallel tracks, never arriving at
an edge or a terminus.
CODE: F:loop N:clustered b~ D:recursive P:med2D
NOTE: `gradient` was considered for N because the boundary feels permeable, but
the structure's identity is generated internally, by its own self-referential
redrawing, rather than by relation to an exterior.

WORD: JOY
RAW: A sphere that keeps exceeding its own radius — not exploding, but
continuously and smoothly renegotiating its boundary outward. The surface
is always slightly ahead of where it just was. There is no preferred direction;
expansion is isotropic.
CODE: F:vector N:gradient b∅ D:drifting P:med2D
NOTE: `loop` was briefly considered — joy can cycle — but the attractor is
not return, it is ongoing outward displacement with no fixed terminus.

WORD: EXCITEMENT
RAW: A taut line that keeps subdividing — each segment angles sharply away
from the last, forming a structure that reaches forward in rapid, uneven
steps. The overall trajectory is directional, but the path itself is
jagged, each node a small compression before the next release.
CODE: F:vector N:contrastive b# D:recursive P:sp1D
NOTE: The boundary is `b#` because excitement is defined against a prior
baseline state — its identity requires the contrast of the step up. `drifting`
was considered for D but the structure recurses at each node rather than
wandering without pattern.

WORD: GRIEF
RAW: A large, slow form that folds inward along itself — not to a point,
but into dense, overlapping layers, each plane lying on the last. The
folding has no final destination; it continues without terminal compression.
The structure is heavy with its own accumulated surface.
CODE: F:convergence N:isolated b# D:recursive P:den3D
NOTE: `collapse` considered, but collapse implies a terminus — grief's
attractor is ongoing inward folding without arrival. `terminal` considered
for D but grief recurses; it revisits its own layers rather than extinguishing.

WORD: SADNESS
RAW: A gentle, wide dome that is slowly losing its curvature — flattening
by degrees, not breaking. It has too much interior volume for its surface
to hold taut. The form sags along its own horizontal axis, spreading
outward as it descends.
CODE: F:collapse N:gradient b~ D:drifting P:med2D
NOTE: The collapse here is slow and directionless rather than convergent —
a distributed yielding rather than a pull toward a point. `drifting` fits
the attractor: no recursion, no terminus, just gradual flattening.

WORD: CONTENT
RAW: A closed volume that sits exactly inside its own boundary — no
pressure outward, no inward pull. The edges are neither advancing nor
retreating. It occupies its own space with a kind of geometric completeness,
as though the form and its container have reached an exact fit.
CODE: F:loop N:clustered b~ D:stable P:med2D
NOTE: `point` was considered — there is something minimal about contentment —
but the form has interior extent, it isn't dimensionless. The loop here is
closed and still, not cycling; it represents equilibrium rather than motion.

WORD: PEACEFUL
RAW: A plane extending in all directions without encountering resistance or
an edge. It has no centre of mass, no preferred axis. Objects at rest on it
remain at rest without any force to disturb them. The structure is less a
thing than an absence of differential.
CODE: F:point N:gradient b∅ D:stable P:med2D
NOTE: This was the hardest assignment. The form resists all F values — it is
almost formless — but `point` captures the zero-differential quality better
than the others: no extension, no return, no collapse. The contradiction is
that the RAW describes a plane while the code assigns `point`; I read the
plane as the *neighbourhood* and the absence of differential as the *form*.
P:med2D reflects the spatial extent of the neighbourhood.

WORD: DESPERATION
RAW: Many vectors originating from a single compressed locus, all firing
outward simultaneously — but each terminates abruptly against an invisible
boundary and snaps back. The structure is all radial thrust and immediate
return, the spokes of a wheel that keep trying to escape the rim and cannot.
CODE: F:convergence N:contrastive b# D:recursive P:den2D
NOTE: `collapse` considered, since the returning vectors compress back toward
origin. But the attractor is the failed-escape cycle, not the compression
itself — so `convergence` with `recursive` captures the locked radial pattern
better. The boundary is sharply defined by its resistance.

WORD: TERRIFIED
RAW: Instantaneous: a form that was volumetric becomes a single dimensionless
puncture. There is no approach, no visible contraction — the structure simply
ceases to occupy space except at one location, with total rigidity. Nothing
moves. The form is a held, frozen singularity.
CODE: F:point N:isolated b# D:stable P:sp0D
NOTE: FEAR and TERRIFIED share F:point and D:stable, which seems right —
they are the same attractor at different magnitudes. TERRIFIED's N is more
emphatically `isolated b#` because the boundary is absolute and admits nothing;
FEAR at least retained traces of an exterior. The distinction is intensity
of enclosure, not structural type.

WORD: PANICKED
RAW: The structure has no stable axis — it keeps generating new vectors
perpendicular to the last, each one full-force, each one immediately
abandoned for another. The overall form resembles a rapid, irregular
expansion of failed trajectories, each leaving a trace that bends
away from the previous. No two lines are parallel; nothing completes.
CODE: F:vector N:contrastive b# D:recursive P:den2D
NOTE: Contrasted with EXCITEMENT: both use `vector` and `recursive`, but
EXCITEMENT's vectors are sequential and directional while PANIC's are
simultaneous and self-cancelling. The `contrastive b#` reflects how each
vector defines itself against the failed one before it. `collapse` was
considered but the structure expands, not contracts — it is too much
directionality, not too little volume.
