NOTE: Tempted by "collapse" for F, but the attractor holds — fear at rest 
is not yet collapsing, it is braced. The sharp boundary is essential; fear 
knows exactly where it ends and the threat begins.



WORD: ANXIETY
RAW: A loop that doesn't quite close — each pass falls slightly short of 
or slightly past the previous one, producing a slow orbital drift that never 
settles. It occupies more space than it should for what it is. The centre 
of the loop is not empty but it is also not reachable.
CODE: F:loop N:gradient b~ D:recursive P:med2D
NOTE: The non-closing loop makes "recursive" the clear choice over "drifting" 
— it is not going anywhere, it is returning, imprecisely. Gradient neighbourhood 
because anxiety has no clean edge; it bleeds into adjacent states.



WORD: JOY
RAW: A rapid expansion from a centre point outward in all directions 
simultaneously — not an explosion, because it isn't violent, but a 
steady pressurised bloom. The structure is almost spherical but it keeps 
growing in discrete pulses rather than continuously. There is no clear outer 
boundary; it simply becomes less dense as it moves outward.
CODE: F:vector N:clustered b∅ D:stable P:den3D
NOTE: Vector over convergence because the movement is outward, not inward. 
The attractor for joy is the bloom-state itself, not its origin or its edge.



WORD: EXCITEMENT
RAW: A vector with a pronounced forward lean — almost unstable in its 
directionality, like a structure balanced on a narrow leading edge. It 
has length but not much width; it is aerodynamic rather than expansive. 
There is vibration along the shaft of it, small lateral oscillations that 
don't disrupt the forward drive.
CODE: F:vector N:contrastive b# D:stable P:sp1D
NOTE: Contrastive because excitement is defined against what it is moving 
toward — it needs a target to have an identity. Sharper boundary than joy; 
excitement knows what it is not.



WORD: GRIEF
RAW: A convergence that has already arrived — everything has been drawn 
inward and now sits compressed at a point that is too small for what it 
contains. There is no movement but the structure is under enormous internal 
pressure. It does not radiate outward; it holds.
CODE: F:convergence N:isolated b# D:stable P:sp0D
NOTE: Isolated because grief at its attractor is not defined relationally — 
it is self-contained, sealed. The sharp boundary is not defensive but 
structural: grief doesn't leak, it accumulates.



WORD: SADNESS
RAW: A gentle downward curvature, like a surface that has lost the tension 
holding it flat. It doesn't fall, it settles — a slow redistribution 
of weight toward the lowest available point. The structure is wide and 
low rather than tall; it spreads horizontally as it descends.
CODE: F:vector N:gradient b~ D:drifting P:med2D
NOTE: Vector over collapse because sadness has directionality without 
terminal endpoint — it moves but does not arrive at nothing. Drifting 
for the same reason; the attractor is the descent itself, not a final state.



WORD: CONTENT
RAW: A closed form with no particular orientation — it doesn't point 
anywhere or press against anything. Slightly rounded, consistently proportioned.

CODE: F:loop N:clustered b~ D:stable P:med2D
NOTE: Loop rather than point because content has duration and self-reference
