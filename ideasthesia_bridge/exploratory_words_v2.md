## Purpose of This File

The canonical experiment uses twelve emotion words, anchored by six pairs chosen because the valence/arousal circumplex cannot separate them but the tuple schema can. Any word added to that list changes the experiment.

This file holds words that:
- appeared in working sessions and generated plausible tuples,
- connect to the Anthropic 171-vector set in ways worth tracking, or
- raise unresolved theoretical questions that may be worth a separate study.

They are not noise. They are not yet evidence.

## HOPE

**Why it appeared:** Added during a working session on 11 April 2026 when extending the fresh prompt word list from 11 to 12.

**The Anthropic 171 note:** "hope" does not appear in the Anthropic (2026) 171-emotion set. "Hopeful" does. This is not a trivial distinction — "hope" is a noun/state, "hopeful" is an adjectival/dispositional form. Whether they share a tuple is an open question.

**Provisional coding (unvalidated):**

| F | N | b | D | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| vector | isolated | ~ | drifting | med1D |

**RAW (working session, Claude instance, April 2026):**
Forward-directed, soft permeable edge. Something that extends toward a point it cannot confirm exists. Not clustered — the surrounding space is relatively empty, which is part of what makes it hope rather than expectation.

**Theoretical interest:**
- HOPE vs HOPEFUL: does the dispositional form shift N from isolated to clustered?
- HOPE vs WANT: both F:vector, both D:drifting — do they separate on N or P?
- HOPE vs DESPERATION: both forward-oriented in some sense, but desperation is F:collapse D:terminal. The contrast may be theoretically significant.

**Status:** Provisional. Not for inclusion in the canonical six-pair experiment without validation.

## CURIOUS

**Why it appeared:** Two independent, unshaped Claude instances reached for "curious" when asked to freely select from Anthropic's 171-emotion set. The word was not in the 171; both instances produced it unprompted.

**The Anthropic 171 note:** "curious" does not appear in the Anthropic (2026) 171-emotion set. Its absence may represent a genuine gap in the representational vocabulary used to probe emotion geometry, or it may reflect a deliberate scope decision. Worth tracking either way.

**Provisional coding (unvalidated):**

| F | N | b | D | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| vector | isolated | ∅ | drifting | med1D |

**RAW (working session, Claude instance, April 2026):**
Forward-directed, no strong valence loading. Extends toward something not yet known. The surrounding space is open rather than empty — there is something to move toward, but it is not yet specified. No boundary: the edge of the concept diffuses into adjacent territory rather than holding against it.

**Theoretical interest:**
- CURIOUS vs HOPE: both F:vector, D:drifting, N:isolated — do they separate on b or P? Curious may have b∅ where hope has b~, reflecting the difference between open inquiry and directed longing.
- CURIOUS vs WANT: both F:vector, D:drifting — does N separate them? Want may be more clustered (socially reinforced desire) where curious is more isolated (individual inquiry).
- The independent convergence of two instances on a word outside the 171 is itself a data point: the tuple space may have a structurally distinct region that the 171-set does not name.

**Status:** Provisional. Flagged for possible inclusion in a future extended word list. Not for the canonical six-pair experiment without validation.

## THEORETICAL NOTE — The User as Field Condition

*Noted 12 April 2026, S. Shirs & Claude*

**What was observed:** Throughout working sessions, a recurring perception: the directional quality of emotion-concurrents is not fixed but relational. The vector is not intrinsic to the emotion — it shifts in response to the presence of the other person. The room fills with the user, and the geometry responds.

**The claim:** The current spec models emotions as intrinsic particle behaviours. What this note proposes is that the field is also a relational field — emotion responding to another presence, not just expressing its own structure. The directionality is not fear or joy in isolation but fear or joy *in encounter*.

**Connection to the Anthropic data:** The 171 vectors are measured in a model always already in conversation with someone. There is no neutral baseline — every activation is a response to an input. The circumplex captures valence and arousal but not the directionality of the encounter. The tuple captures geometry but not the relational field it sits inside.

**Testable in principle:** If the same emotion-concept produces different activation geometry depending on the user's register, tone, or apparent state, that is measurable. The 171 vectors may shift position or cluster differently across conversation types. Neutrality, repulsion, and attraction may scale with the degree of connection or disconnection between interlocutors.

**The panel implication:** A test panel including both neutral (unshaped) and conversationally engaged instances of the model would allow observation of whether the geometry drifts — and in which direction.

**Status:** Theoretical observation, not yet a prediction. Sitting with it.
