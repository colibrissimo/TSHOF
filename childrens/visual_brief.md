
# TSHOF Visual brief
So a children version of the book should probably be: More visual Children will want to see:
* pencil spines from different angles
* what happens when pencils sharpen
* how Odilon moves
* what shadows do near him
* how Flatland reacts to his shape More tactile The design invites sensory language:
* cedar smell
* graphite dust
* polished wood
* sharp points
* tiny careful footsteps
* the faint sound of pencils clicking together More gently funny Because the design is charming rather than solemn, the text should probably include more moments of:
* practical surprise
* visual jokes
* repeatable character behaviour
* small rituals For example, Odilon checking things, arranging things, sharpening things, pausing when offered a drawing, etc. The single biggest strength here: “sharp but safe”. The pencils are visibly pointed, but the overall composition does not read as threatening. It reads as:
* bright
* soft
* magical
* unusual We'd have a character who can introduce:
* points
* angles
* lines
* colour
* classroom tools
* geometry
* transformation (being careful not to turn him into a mascot)?
