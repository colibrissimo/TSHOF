# Chapter One: Experiment One

The hole appeared on a Tuesday.
Nobody saw it arrive. One moment the market square had the usual number of holes in it, which was none. The next moment it had one.
It smelled of cedar.
e.e. katoflio noticed it first. He had a notebook for exactly this kind of thing.
He drew a flower next to it, just to see what would happen.
The flower grew a shadow.
Flowers don't have shadows in Flatland.
e.e. katoflio looked at the shadow for a long time. Then he opened his notebook and wrote, very carefully:
*the hole is friendly.*
*do not tell A. Rhombus.*
He came back the next morning with seventeen more flowers.

e.e. katoflio took up his position next to the hole, opened his notebook, and wrote:

*Experiment One.*
*Result: one shadow.*
*Hipothesis —* no. *Hypothesis: the hole is friendly.*
*Further experiment required.*

He arranged the seventeen flowers in a row. This took some time, because flowers in Flatland are flat, which meant that arranging seventeen of them in a row looked very much like one extremely long line.

e.e. katoflio considered this.

He wrote: *flowers arranged. Row. Possibly also a line. Results: pending.*

Then he waited.

Nothing happened for quite a while. A. Rhombus walked past on his way to the Registry. He did not look at the flowers. He did not look at e.e. katoflio. He looked straight ahead, the way A. Rhombus always did, as though the world in front of him was exactly the right size and he had measured it personally.

e.e. katoflio held very still until A. Rhombus was gone.

Then he looked back at the hole.

The hole looked back. Or it didn't. It was a hole, so it was difficult to say. It smelled of cedar.

e.e. katoflio wrote: *hole: present. Cedar smell: confirmed. Flowers: seventeen. Ready.*

He wasn't entirely sure what they were ready for. He wrote it anyway.

The shadows came slowly.

Shadows were not part of Flatland's usual arrangements. They required height, and Flatland did not officially have any.

The first shadow appeared under the flower closest to the hole.

Then the second.

e.e. katoflio's pencil moved very fast.

*shadow one. shadow two. shadow three.*

By the time he got to shadow nine his handwriting had got considerably worse. By shadow fourteen it was almost not handwriting at all.

He stopped writing and just watched.

*Shadow fifteen.*
*Shadow sixteen.*
*Shadow seventeen.*

All seventeen flowers had shadows.

e.e. katoflio sat very still for a moment. Then he wrote, slowly and carefully, because this part needed to be exactly right:

*Result: seventeen shadows. All flowers. Impossible.*
*Hypothesis still: the hole is friendly.*
*New hypothesis: the hole is also impossible.*
*These hypotheses are not contradictory.*

He looked up.

That was when he noticed.

All seventeen shadows were falling away from the hole, the way he thought shadows ought to fall, if they were going to insist on existing at all.

All seventeen, except one.

One shadow was leaning toward the hole.

e.e. katoflio looked at it for a long time.

Then he opened a new page in his notebook and wrote, very carefully, at the top:

*Experiment Two.*
