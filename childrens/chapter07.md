# Chapter Seven: Alone

e.e. katoflio came back on a Thursday.

He had not told A. Octagon. He had not decided not to tell her; it was more that the decision had already been made by the time he realised it, the way sometimes you understand you have been thinking about something for several days before you admit that you have been thinking about it.

He had his notebook. He had his pencil. He stood at the edge of the market square and wrote:

*Day 6. Solo return. Conditions: ordinary. Purpose: observation.*

He looked at what he had written.

He crossed out *observation*.


The hole was there. The cedar smell was there. The traders were trading, and no one was paying attention to a boy with a notebook standing near an irregularity in the pavement.

e.e. katoflio walked to the hole and stood beside it and did not feel like a scientist.

He felt, instead, like someone who has come back to a place because the place had been in his thoughts and he had wanted to see whether it was still there. That was a reasonable thing to feel. It was also not the same as an experiment.

He opened his notebook to a blank page.


Here was the thing he had not been able to do in front of A. Octagon, not because A. Octagon would have laughed (she would not have laughed) but because some things need to be done without being observed, before they can become real. He understood this the way he understood most things: imprecisely at first, and then all at once.

He drew himself.

Just: himself. A small Isosceles, roughly correct, with a notebook tucked under one angle.

It was not a good drawing. That was fine. It was not meant to be a good drawing. It was meant to be him.

He tore it carefully along the edge and placed it beside the hole.

Not at the hole's edge. Not as a test. Just near it, where something coming from the other side might see it, if something could see.

Then he sat down, which was not methodical at all, and he waited.


The square continued to be the square. The traders continued to trade. The hole continued to smell of cedar and to look like a hole.


e.e. katoflio was aware, sitting there, of how different this was from standing and holding a notebook and treating the irregularity as a problem to be solved. Problems were comfortable. Problems had method. Sitting beside a hole because you had come back because you wanted to had rather less method in it, and rather more of everything else.

He opened his notebook.

*Day 6,* he had written. *Purpose: observation.*

He crossed out *observation* again, more firmly. He wrote:

*He thought about what to write and did not write it.*

This was accurate.


The cedar smell changed.

Not stronger. But different in the way that a room changes when someone in it has turned to look at you.

e.e. katoflio went still.

The smell was moving. Not drifting, not spreading, but moving with the quality of something that knew where it was going. It moved across the pavement toward the drawing, the small imprecise Isosceles with the notebook tucked under its angle, and it settled there, the way a presence settles when it has found what it was looking for.

It stayed.


e.e. katoflio did not move. He was eleven and he had a scientific mind and he was also, at this moment, not breathing.

Something on the other side had looked at his drawing.

Not at the hole. Not at the experiments. Not at the seventeen flowers or the careful measurements or the returned eye. At the drawing he had made of himself because he had wanted to, on a Thursday, alone.

Then the cedar smell eased back, gently, the way you might step back after looking at something closely. Not withdrawing, just giving space, as though it understood that being looked at required a moment to recover from.

He picked up the drawing. Nothing had changed. It was still a small Isosceles, roughly correct, with a notebook.

He put it in his pocket.

He wrote in his notebook:

*It knew I was here.*

He looked at this for a moment.

Then, underneath:

*I think it was trying not to frighten me.*

He closed the notebook.


---


He carried the drawing for four days.

It was not a talisman, not evidence, not a lucky charm. It was simply in his pocket, and he was aware of it.

He did not go back to the square. He went to lessons, ate his meals, did his work. He did not tell A. Octagon about Thursday.

On Monday, A. Octagon told him the review window. Observations due Wednesday morning. Which meant Tuesday was the last day the square would be simply a square, before it became evidence.

He did not decide to go back on Tuesday. The decision had already happened.


---


He was at the square before the traders had finished setting up.

The rope was there. The cordon, the posts, the measuring rod at its precise angle. The hole was inside it, smelling of cedar, looking like a hole.

e.e. katoflio did not go inside the rope.

He sat down outside it, at the nearest point the rope allowed, in approximately the place he had sat on Thursday. He opened his notebook and wrote:

*Day 11. Tuesday. Window.*

He did not write anything else.


He took the drawing out of his pocket.

He did not place it as an experiment. He did not measure the distance from the hole or note the position in his notebook. He set it on the ground in front of him, facing the hole, and left it there.

Then he waited.

The square did what squares do. The traders traded. The cedar smell was present, ambient, giving nothing away.

Nothing happened.

e.e. katoflio sat with this. He was aware, in a way he had not been on Thursday, that nothing happening was not the same as nothing being there. He had learned that. It had taken six experiments and one unwitnessed Thursday and eight days of a rope cordon to learn it, but he had.

He stayed.


It was not the cedar smell that changed first.

It was the quality of the air near his hand, the hand that was resting on the ground beside the drawing, which became, very gradually and without announcement, warm.

Not the warmth of sunlight, which moves and shifts and belongs to the sky. This was still. Directed. The warmth of something that has come close and is choosing to remain close.

e.e. katoflio did not move his hand.

On Thursday, the cedar had gone to the drawing. It had looked at what he made.

This was not that.

This was not the drawing. This was his hand. Whatever was on the other side had crossed the distance not to the picture of him but to him, and it was staying.

He thought: it came back.

And then, because he was eleven and precise even about the things that undid him: not back. Here. It came *here*, to where I am, and it stayed.


The warmth did not press or insist. It was simply there, at the edge of his hand, with the quality of something that is being careful about how much it offers because it does not want to ask too much in return.

He held very still, because holding still was the one thing he could give back.

He stayed like that for a long time. The traders traded. The cedar smell settled around them both. The drawing lay between them on the ground, a small Isosceles with a notebook, and whatever was beside his hand regarded it, or regarded him, or both. He could not tell the difference, and was beginning to think that was correct.

At some point the warmth eased back, gently, the way it had before. Not gone. Just no longer close.

e.e. katoflio let out a breath he had not known he was holding.


He picked up the drawing. He put it back in his pocket. He opened his notebook.

He thought about what to write. He thought for a while.

Then he wrote:

*He came because he wanted to.*

He looked at this.

He crossed out *He* and wrote *It*, and then crossed that out too and wrote *He* again, because that was the more accurate word and he had promised himself, when he started the notebook, that the notebook would be accurate.

He closed it. He stood up. He looked at the hole, which was inside its rope, which smelled of cedar, which was the ordinary market square of Flatland on a Tuesday morning in the time before the review had begun.

He knew something now that he had not known on Thursday, and could not prove, and would not be able to put in any form that the Provost's ledger would accept, and which was, for exactly those reasons, entirely his.
