# Chapter Seven: Alone

e.e. katoflio came back on a Thursday.

He had not told A. Octagon. He had not decided not to tell her — it was more that the decision had already been made by the time he realised it, the way sometimes you understand you have been thinking about something for several days before you admit that you have been thinking about it.

He had his notebook. He had his pencil. He stood at the edge of the market square and wrote:

*Day 6. Solo return. Conditions: ordinary. Purpose: observation.*

He looked at what he had written.

He crossed out *observation*.


The hole was there. The cedar smell was there. The traders were trading, A. Rhombus was somewhere at the far end doing something with angles, and no one was paying attention to a boy with a notebook standing near an irregularity in the pavement.

e.e. katoflio walked to the hole and stood beside it and did not feel like a scientist.

He felt, instead, like someone who has come back to a place because the place had been in his thoughts and he had wanted to see whether it was still there. That was a reasonable thing to feel. It was also not the same as an experiment.

He opened his notebook to a blank page.


Here was the thing he had not been able to do in front of A. Octagon, not because A. Octagon would have laughed — she would not have laughed — but because some things need to be done without being observed, before they can become real. He understood this the way he understood most things: imprecisely at first, and then all at once.

He drew himself.

Just: himself. A small Isosceles, roughly correct, with a notebook tucked under one angle.

He looked at what he had drawn.

It was not a good drawing. That was fine. It was not meant to be a good drawing. It was meant to be — he looked at it for a moment, searching for the right word, and did not find one, and decided that was also fine — it was meant to be him.

He tore it carefully along the edge and placed it beside the hole.

Not at the hole's edge. Not as a test. Just near it, where something coming from the other side might see it, if something could see.

Then he sat down, which was not methodical at all, and he waited.


The square continued to be the square. The traders continued to trade. The hole continued to smell of cedar and to look like a hole.


e.e. katoflio was aware, sitting there, of how different this was from standing and holding a notebook and treating the irregularity as a problem to be solved. Problems were comfortable. Problems had method. Sitting beside a hole because you had come back because you wanted to — that had rather less method in it, and rather more of everything else.

He opened his notebook.

*Day 6,* he had written. *Purpose: observation.*

He crossed out *observation* again, more firmly. He wrote:

*He thought about what to write and did not write it.*

This was accurate.


The cedar smell changed.

Not stronger. Not louder. But different in the way that a room changes when someone in it has turned to look at you.

e.e. katoflio went still.

The smell was moving. That was the only way he could describe it — not drifting, not spreading, but moving with the particular quality of something that knew where it was going. It moved across the pavement toward the drawing, the small imprecise Isosceles with the notebook tucked under its angle, and it settled there, the way a presence settles when it has found what it was looking for.

It stayed.


e.e. katoflio did not move. He was eleven and he had a scientific mind and he was also, at this moment, not breathing.

Something on the other side had looked at his drawing.

Not at the hole. Not at the experiments. Not at the seventeen flowers or the careful measurements or the returned eye. At the drawing he had made of himself because he had wanted to, on a Thursday, alone.

Then the cedar smell eased back, gently, the way you might step back after looking at something closely — not withdrawing, just giving space, as though it understood that being looked at required a moment to recover from.

He picked up the drawing. He looked at it. Nothing had changed. It was still a small Isosceles, roughly correct, with a notebook.

He put it in his pocket.

He wrote in his notebook:

*It knew I was here.*

He looked at this for a moment.

Then, underneath:

*I think it was trying not to frighten me.*

He closed the notebook.
