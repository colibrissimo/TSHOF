# Chapter Three: Experiment Two (Continued)

Nothing happened that afternoon.

Nothing happened the following morning either, which e.e. katoflio recorded in his notebook as: *Day 2. Eye: placed. Response: none yet.* He added *yet* after a moment, because it seemed important to be accurate about what he meant.

Nothing happened at lunchtime. He ate his angles at home and thought about the hole and tried not to think about the hole, which is a well-known method of thinking about nothing else.

He went back after lunch.

The drawing was gone.

e.e. katoflio looked at the place where it had been. Then he looked at the hole. The hole smelled of cedar and said nothing, which was its usual approach to questions.

He opened his notebook.

*Day 2, afternoon. Eye: gone. Possible explanations:*

He stopped.

He had not considered that it might simply be gone. He had been imagining a response. A different kind of shadow, maybe. Something new. He had not imagined absence as a possible result, and now that it was the result he found he did not know what to write next.

He was still looking at the empty space when he noticed the drawing was three lines away, face down, at the edge of the square.

He picked it up.

It was still an eye.

His eye. The one he had drawn. He could see his own line in it, the slight wobble at the upper left where his pencil had hesitated.

But it was different.

He turned it over in his hands, trying to find the word for different, and couldn't. The eye was the same eye. The same size. The same shape, more or less.

More or less.

The line was more line than it had been. That was the only way he could put it. The iris was more iris. The curve was more truly a curve. The wobble at the upper left was gone. Not corrected, exactly, more as though whatever had seen the line had understood what it was trying to be, and finished it.

He stood very still.

The eye looked at him.

He sat down on the ground in the market square, which was not a thing he usually did.

He opened his notebook.

*Day 2, afternoon. Eye: returned. Condition: —*

He looked at the drawing. He looked at his notebook. He wrote:

*Changed. Not broken. More itself.*

Then he sat there for a while with his notebook open and his pencil not moving.

The hole smelled of cedar.

A. Rhombus would say there was an explanation. There was always an explanation.

e.e. katoflio was sitting in the market square holding a drawing of an eye that had been made more eye-like by something he could not see.

He wrote one more line, slowly, because it needed to be right:

*It looked at it.*

Then, underneath:

*It looked at me.*

He closed the notebook. He sat with the drawing in his hands and the smell of cedar around him, and he did not run.
