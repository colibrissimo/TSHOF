# Editorial Plan: The Sharpened History of Flatland (Children's Adaptation)
## Part One — Structural Revision

*Written March 2026, fourth instance. Based on full manuscript read and collaborative review with Sarah.*

---

## Target audience (settled)

- **Shelf position:** 9–12
- **Core reader:** 10–12, reading independently
- **Comp territory:** Norton Juster, Rebecca Stead, T.H. White — precise, dry, trusting of children's intelligence
- **Not:** Roald Dahl, not a picture book, not an early chapter book

The voice is the engine. Precision, restraint, dry comedy, interior recognition. These are not surface choices to be simplified; they are the book. Do not revise downward.

### Merged chapters

**New Chapter 7: Alone** (merges old Ch7 + Ch9)

Built around the solo return and "He came because he wanted to."

- **First movement:** e.e. returns alone on Thursday. Draws himself (the small Isosceles). The cedar smell moves toward the drawing. Notebook: *It knew I was here. / I think it was trying not to frighten me.*
- **Second movement:** e.e. returns on Tuesday — the last day before the review. Places the drawing again. Warmth arrives at his hand, not the hole's edge: directed, personal, chosen. Notebook: crosses out *He*, writes *It*, crosses out, writes *He* again.
- **Key craft note:** The notebook entries must escalate, not repeat. "It knew I was here" is recognition. "He came because he wanted to" is choice. The chapter's arc is from *being seen* to *being chosen*. Do not let the two visits feel like the same encounter twice.
- **What to cut:** The repeated "cedar smell changed / not stronger but present" setup. Use it once in this chapter. The second visit should arrive differently — perhaps through the warmth first, not the smell.

**New Chapter 8: Under Review** (merges old Ch8 + Ch10)

Built around the rope, the wrong language, the measuring rod, and A. Octagon's decision.

- **First movement:** The rope, the cordon, the functionary with his ledger. "Surface anomaly" vs "temporary irregularity pending classification." A. Octagon refuses both on accuracy. The measuring rod is inside the cordon.
- **The irreversible change:** The measuring rod has been sharpened. The Provost's own instrument — the thing the institution brought to measure the mystery — has been made more precise than it was designed to be. This is comic (bureaucracy's tools betray it), irreversible (you cannot un-sharpen an instrument), and foreshadows Ch9 where the Provost himself is touched. e.e. notices. Records it.
- **Second movement:** A. Octagon writing the observations. e.e. tells her about Tuesday — the warmth, the *He*. She decides to write the accurate sentence. During the writing, a faint cedar smell arrives at the Registry — not the warmth, not the presence, just the smell, briefly. The phenomenon has noticed it is being written about accurately. This is the whisper of C: the boundaries are porous, but the square remains the charged place.
- **Key craft note:** This chapter has the most moving parts. It needs the functionary, the measuring rod discovery, the Provost's brief appearance, the scene at the Registry. Sequence carefully: square first (rope, functionary, rod), then Registry (writing, cedar whisper). Two locations, one chapter, clear transition.

## Syntax revision targets

These apply across the whole manuscript during line-editing, not just the merged chapters.

**1. "The particular [noun] of someone who [clause]"**
This construction belongs to A. Octagon. It characterises her way of being in the world. Use it once per chapter maximum, and only in her presence. Do not give it to the narrator generally. Currently appears in: Ch4, Ch5, Ch6, Ch8, Ch9, Ch10, Ch12. Cut to: Ch4, Ch5, Ch8 (merged), Ch10.

**2. "He/she was quiet for a moment."**
Pick the 3–4 silences that are structurally load-bearing. Cut the rest. Let white space do the work the sentence is currently doing.

**3. "Which was [explanation of Flatland convention]"**
The parenthetical world-building insertion. Brilliant in Ch1, still working in Ch6. By the later chapters, the reader knows this world. Trust them. Drop explanatory insertions from Ch7 onward.

**4. A. Rhombus appearances**
Keep in Ch1 (establishes the world) and Ch10/Odilon (counterpoint to the naming — his obliviousness is the final joke). Remove from all middle chapters. His absence makes the bookend appearances sharper.

## What the revision is NOT

- It is not a downward age revision. The voice is correct for 10+.
- It is not a POV change. e.e. katoflio is the right centre of consciousness.
- It is not an Odilon book. It is an e.e. book about learning to say hello. Odilon is the mystery, not the protagonist.
- It is not a rewrite of Chapters 1–6 or 9–10 (new numbering). Those are done.

## Revision order

1. Write the merged Chapter 7 (Alone). This is the most delicate merge because both original chapters are emotionally strong; the task is escalation without repetition.
2. Write the merged Chapter 8 (Under Review). This is the most complex merge because it introduces the measuring rod change and the cedar whisper, and has the most moving parts.
3. Line-edit Chapters 1–6 for syntax targets only (no structural changes).
4. Line-edit Chapters 9–10 (new numbering) for syntax targets and to ensure the Provost's arc reads correctly with the measuring rod already established.
5. Update chapter numbering, filenames, and all reference documents.
