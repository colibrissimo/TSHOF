**Chapter Two: Experiment Two**

**Chapter Three: Experiment Two (Continued)**

**Chapter Four: A. Octagon**
e.e. katoflio finds A. Octagon outside the Registry and shows her the drawing. She looks at it properly — asks about the wobble, notices the line. Does not explain. Does not dismiss. Asks: *Will you take me there?* Ends on: *strange and correct.*

**Chapter Five: The Square**
They return together. The hole is ordinary at first. A. Octagon reaches toward the edge. e.e. glimpses something small and careful at the limit of the light. A. Octagon feels warmth. Her pencil has been sharpened — finer than any point had a right to be. e.e. finds the word: *careful.* Ends on: *neither of them said anything else, because there was nothing else that needed saying yet.*

## The register: what you must protect

Deadpan first. Lyric second. The comedy carries the emotion.
Short sentences. White space works. Notebook entries as structural rhythm.
Do not explain what the prose already shows.
Do not paint Odilon — name him, let the reader fill him in.
The eye stays doodle-sized. The cedar stays a smell, not a symbol.

These phrases are load-bearing. Use them sparingly, as anchors:

## Chapter Six: fully planned, not yet written

The beat sheet exists in the conversation. Here is the essential:

**What it must do:** consequence, not another reveal. The impossible has been earned. Now show what it costs to carry it.

**Emotional movement:** shared care → protected truth under scrutiny.

**The Provost arrives.** He is a large Dodecagon, comic obstacle, entirely serious in his own mind. He is not a villain. His power comes from procedure. He has noticed irregularity in the market square and requires official accounting of it.

**A. Octagon's role:** custodian of truth under pressure. She does not lie. She protects by refusing to simplify. Her courage lives in exactness, not rebellion.

**e.e. katoflio learns:** truth needs defending from the wrong kind of language. He watches A. Octagon choose precision over panic and learns something active about carrying knowledge.

**Three dangers:**
1. Do not make the Provost a villain — obstructive by nature of office, not cruelty
2. Do not let A. Octagon explain too much — she narrows, she does not lecture
3. Do not make e.e. passive — he must learn something, even if he says little

**The unforgettable moment:** the Provost asks for clarity and A. Octagon refuses him not by rebellion, but by exactness. That is very Flatland, very her, and the chapter's centre.

**Endpoint:** the square is no longer privately theirs. The truth has not been exposed, but it has entered danger. A. Octagon telling e.e. quietly that they must be more careful now — or e.e. realising the next visit cannot be innocent in the old way.
