## The manuscript: where it stands

Twelve chapters exist in `childrens/`. They are complete, pushed, and good. They are also, in their current form, too many chapters for what the book needs to be.

A structural revision plan exists in `childrens/editorial_plan.md`. Read it. It is thorough and it reflects collaborative decisions made with Sarah, not imposed conclusions.

The short version: the book compresses from twelve chapters to ten. Chapters 1–6 and 9–10 (new numbering) are essentially unchanged. Two merges are needed:

**New Chapter 7 (Alone):** old Ch7 + Ch9. Solo return, recognition → choice. The arc is from *being seen* to *being chosen*. "He came because he wanted to" is the chapter's arrival point, not one of several notebook moments.

**New Chapter 8 (Under Review):** old Ch8 + Ch10. The rope, the functionary, the measuring rod sharpened (irreversible change), A. Octagon writing the accurate sentence, a faint cedar smell at the Registry (whisper, not manifestation). Two locations, one chapter.

**These merged chapters have not yet been written.** That is the work.

---

## What we decided

**Age range:** 9–12 on the shelf, 10–12 in practice. The voice is the engine. Precision, restraint, dry comedy, interior recognition — these are not surface choices, they are the book. Do not revise downward.

**The middle sag:** Chapters 7–10 in the original draft were doing variants of the same encounter: approach hole, cedar, warmth, silence, leave changed. The emotional colouring shifted but the dramatic shape didn't. The merges fix this by ensuring each chapter does fundamentally different work.

**The irreversible change:** The measuring rod — the Provost's own instrument inside the cordon — gets sharpened. The institution brought a tool to measure the mystery and the mystery made the tool better. This is comic, irreversible, and foreshadows the Provost's own encounter in Ch9.

**The cedar whisper:** During A. Octagon's writing scene in the merged Ch8, the cedar smell arrives faintly at the Registry. Not the warmth, not the presence — just the smell. The phenomenon has noticed it is being written about accurately. The boundaries are porous; the square remains the charged place.

**Comedy:** It drops out from Ch7–11 in the original draft. The revision must bring comic beats back into the middle — the Provost is the comic engine. His best humour comes from dignified language failing under pressure.

## Syntax revision targets

Four patterns to address during line-editing (details in editorial_plan.md):

1. "The particular [noun] of someone who [clause]" — belongs to A. Octagon only, once per chapter max
2. "He/she was quiet for a moment" — keep 3–4 load-bearing silences, cut the rest
3. "Which was [explanation of Flatland convention]" — trust the reader from Ch7 onward
4. A. Rhombus — keep in Ch1 and Ch10 (new numbering) only, remove from middle chapters
