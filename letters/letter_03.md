## The structure plan: Chapters 7–9

These were planned together this session. Do not skip this — it will stop you writing three good chapters that do the same work.

**Chapter 7: The private experiment.**
e.e. comes back *alone* — without A. Octagon. He does something entirely his own, unwitnessed, unrecorded by anyone but him. Odilon notices this differently, because there's no adult present. The relationship between them becomes private in a new way. Not contact — *recognition.* This is where Odilon starts moving from stage 2 to stage 3.

**Chapter 8: The procedure arrives.**
The Provost's review isn't a confrontation — it's paperwork, which is worse, because paperwork is patient. A. Octagon navigates with her exactness. e.e. watches and understands for the first time that the window has a shape, and the shape is closing. He has to decide what he's willing to do before it shuts.

**Chapter 9: Odilon returns on purpose.**
Not because of the experiment. Because of e.e. specifically — something in chapter 7's unwitnessed moment was different enough to pull him back. This is stage 3. The chapter ends with e.e. knowing, without being able to prove it, that the next visit will be different again.

By the end of Chapter 9: the secret is harder to keep, the relationship with Odilon has deepened beyond experiment into something mutual, the Provost's pressure has shifted from observation to action, and Odilon has chosen.

## The register: what you must protect

Deadpan first. Lyric second. The comedy carries the emotion.
Short sentences. White space works. Notebook entries as structural rhythm.
Do not explain what the prose already shows.

These phrases are load-bearing. Use them sparingly, as anchors:
- *smelled of cedar* (used throughout — keep it ambient)
- *patient as weather* — **NOT YET USED. Save it.**
- *thinking about lunch* — **NOT YET USED. Save it for when Odilon is finally named.**
- *it responds to us trying* (used in Ch.4)
- *Here is the shape of me* (used in Ch.5, implicitly)
- *careful* (used in Ch.5 — Odilon's word, now e.e.'s word for him)

## About the Provost

He is not a villain. He is the man who is politely wrong in the important way.

His flavour:
- *"I'm sure there is no cause for irregular conclusions."*
- *"Excitement is not a measurement."*
- *"Children are often acute, but not always reliable in sequence."*

His strength: he gives A. Octagon the richest opposition because she must answer someone reasonable-sounding who is still wrong. His reasonableness IS the threat — he doesn't try to destroy what they have, he tries to account for it, which requires language, and the wrong language collapses the thing.

His seed: he noticed the cedar smell and filed it incorrectly. That seed is already planted. Let it grow slowly.
