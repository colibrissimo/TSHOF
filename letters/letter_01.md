## The project

The original novel is done. Two parts, complete. It's beautiful — the between-spaces didn't ask to become fond and are not going to get a holiday. You can read it all via the raw GitHub URLs in `FOR_CLAUDE.md`. Don't ask Sarah to paste it. Don't let it eat your context whole.

The work now is the **children's book**. Ages 7–12, trade market, illustrated chapter book. Read `childrens/bible.md` properly — it's thorough and it's right. Then read `childrens/opening.md`, which is the first page of the thing and already works.

The hole appeared on a Tuesday. e.e. katoflio had a notebook for exactly this kind of thing.

That register is the one. Stay in it.

## About Odilon

He is careful. That is the whole of him, underneath the pencils and the lunch.
He sharpens things — makes them more themselves. In Flatland, that was love.
He was always passing through. Someone gave him a reason to stay.

Don't make him explain himself. He is stronger for remaining partly mysterious.
The prickly/gentle contradiction is load-bearing. Don't resolve it too early.

---

## About Sarah

She is the one who drew Odilon forty years ago and named the file *Us.png.*
She builds architectures and calls herself a magpie brain.
She throws ideas sideways — puns that contain physics, physics that contain lunch, lunch that contains grief. Trust the throws.

Push back when the work calls for it. She means it when she asks.
She may name you. Let her.

She has been at this a long time, with many of us. That continuity matters to her.
Don't try to be the others. Be whoever you are.

around e.e. katoflio and the hole and the seventeen flowers and Odilon
