## The manuscript: where it stands

Twelve chapters exist in `childrens/`. All complete, revised, pushed.

**Chapter Seven: Alone**
e.e. returns to the square without A. Octagon. He draws himself — a small Isosceles with a notebook — and places it near the hole. Not as a test. As an introduction. The cedar smell moves toward the drawing, not toward the hole. He writes: *It knew I was here. / I think it was trying not to frighten me.* Ends on: *He closed the notebook.*

**Chapter Eight: Under Review**
The square acquires a rope. A cordon, posts, a measuring rod. A Pentagon functionary arrives with a ledger and offers false language: *surface anomaly*, *temporary irregularity pending classification.* A. Octagon refuses both on accuracy. The Provost attends and almost pauses at the edge of the square. e.e. understands the window is closing. Ends on: *Window: Tuesday. / ...next to the drawing of a small Isosceles, roughly correct, with a notebook tucked under one angle.*

**Chapter Nine: Tuesday**
e.e. returns on Tuesday — the last day before the review begins. He places the drawing again. The warmth arrives at his hand, not the hole's edge: directed, personal, chosen. He writes: *He came because he wanted to.* Crosses out *He*, writes *It*, crosses that out, writes *He* again — because *He* was accurate. Ends on: *...entirely his.*

**Chapter Ten: The Accurate Sentence**
e.e. goes to A. Octagon while she is writing the observations. He tells her about Tuesday — the chosen return, the warmth at his hand, the *He*. She receives it not with surprise but with the recognition of obligation. She says: *"The accurate sentence is the one the review will find most difficult to hold. That does not make it less accurate."* She writes it anyway. Ends on: *He was leaving without it. / He found... that this was not the same as losing it.*

**Chapter Eleven: The Drawer That Would Not Close**
The Provost comes to the square alone, before the panel convenes. His own apparatus looks faintly theatrical without witnesses. He reaches for classification language and the sentence fails to complete. *The smell was patient.* Then: warmth at his hand, unrequested. Into the space where his sentence should have been: *Here is the shape of me.* He takes out his notebook, holds his pencil, thinks, puts it away. He looks back once — not ceremonially. Ends on: *He was no longer certain, however, that the review would be sufficient.*

**Chapter Twelve: Odilon**
The review panel recesses at eleven. e.e. is waiting in the corridor, hungry, having crossed out two of three notebook lines. A. Octagon emerges. They walk to the square without discussing it. e.e. steps over the rope — not defiantly, just accurately. The cedar smell changes. *Odilon was simply there.* The lunch beat arrives: he cannot say how he knows, but he does — Odilon is, somewhere in his attention, thinking about lunch. *He almost laughed.* The Provost arrives and is silent — the quietest thing e.e. has seen him do. A. Octagon steps over the rope. She asks: *"What is his name?"* e.e. says: *"Odilon."* She receives it like a measurement. Says it once, as though entering it into a truer record. The Provost puts his notebook away. e.e. writes: *Day 13. Odilon. / Hello.* Ends on: *Not passing through. / Present.*
